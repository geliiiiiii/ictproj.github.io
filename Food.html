and
RESTAURANTS

Sayongsong

This well-known Surigao delicacy is a "kakanin" wrapped in banana leaves that has the color and consistency of a "puto" or rice cake. It’s composed out of glutinous rice, brown sugar, calamansi juice, roasted peanut, and coconut milk. Sayongsong comes out wrapped in banana leaves. Some people say it tastes like “puto” but with coconut milk. This can be easily found in public markets, food stalls, and “pasalubong” shops

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a popular dish in many provinces. The exotic flavor of Surigao's kinilaw distinguishes it from others. Surigao attracts visitors from all over the world and within the country for its delectable kinilaw. This local delicacy is made entirely of raw fish and requires no cooking. Surigaonons prepare this native cuisine by deboning and washing raw fish and combining it with a variety of condiments. This delicacy is a haven for your taste buds because to the right combination of ginger, vinegar, onions, chili peppers, and salt.

Poot-Poot Ginamos

This delicacy is unfamiliar to visitors and even Filipinos coming from Manila. Poot poot ginamos is a unique and delectable Surigao treat. This tasty dish is made of small fishes commonly known as poot poot. This type of fish can only be found off the coast of the Philippines. This fish delicacy includes fermented fish sauce, which is inexpensive, making it even more popular with tourists.

Gigaquit Rhum

Gigaquit Rhum is an established native beverage in Surigao, tasting so good that it ranked fourth on five of the bests of Surigao. Rarely discovered, this one is flavorful and is aged at a tree located in the province called Sasa tree and tastes similar to the taste of “tuba”. Furthermore, because of its low cost and high quality, this is a well-known export product.

Where to Eat in
Siargao

Kermit Siargao Surf Hotel & Restaurant

Located in General Luna at the Kermit Surf Resort, this is an exceptional spot for dinner in Siargao and not to be missed during your visit to the island. The food, which is a blend of Italian and local cuisine, is of the greatest quality and is slightly more costly.
Kermit Siargao has a relaxed beach-style feel with a large, open, traditional pizza oven and serves high-quality local produce such as fresh seafood and a variety of pasta dishes, as well as vegan and vegetarian options.

Bravo Beach Resort

Bravo Restaurant, located in General Luna, serves one of the best lunch menus on Siargao and is right on the beach. Bravo Restaurant offers service and outstanding food, accompanied by beautiful views that can’t be faulted.
They have an excellent tapas meals, along with absolutely stellar burgers, vegan and vegetarian choices, and some of the best mixed drinks on the island.

Mad Monkey Kitchen

Mad Monkey Kitchen is attached to the Mad Monkey Hostel, located north of General Luna just to the west of the Cloud 9 surfing area. At this highly-rated restaurant in Siargao, you can expect beautiful views, a nice quiet location, and delicious food.
serving some of the best pasta and pizza in town as well as vegan and vegetarian options, this is the perfect stop for a peaceful lunch.

La Carinderia

La Carinderia is the go-to place of every island visitor with its chill environment, good music, homey service, and great food from a fusion of Italian cuisine with aFilipino twist. The restaurant also exudes a laid-back, island vibe that lets you dine barefoot on their sandy floor.
