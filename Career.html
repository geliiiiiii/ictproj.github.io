COMMON
OCCUPATIONS

Fisherman

Fishing is a reasonably popular vocation given that they are surrounded by water, but that doesn't imply it is simple. They are in charge of searching out fishing spots, dragging the fish out of the water, correctly storing them so they can be sold, and bringing them back to dry land. In search of the best sites and densest concentration of fish, many boats will spend weeks or even months at sea.

Service Crew

Numerous companies constantly require staff members. Presenting menus to customers is the responsibility of the Service Crew. They handle orders and respond to inquiries about the menu items. They give recommendations and serve food/beverages to consumers. They create the bill, which breaks out the entire cost of the meal and any applicable sales taxes.

Lifeguard

Lifeguards are always needed due to the numerous beaches and resorts. The primary duty of a lifeguard is to stop people from drowning and becoming harmed. Candidates must have the ability to reduce or eliminate potentially dangerous situations or actions in order to ensure the safety of visitors.

Tour Coordinator

Tour Due to the variety of captivating sights, tourists have planners to keep them on schedule. The following are some of the duties of a tour coordinator: Informing clients of each tour's schedule, determining routes based on weather predictions and the duration of each tour, scheduling visits and, if necessary, purchasing tickets in advance for events. Coordinator

Entrepreneur

An entrepreneur is a person who recognizes a business potential and seizes it. The business may operate in any type of sector or service area. Entrepreneurs will develop a business strategy and seek funding as an entrepreneur to finance your enterprise.

Accountant

Staff accountants draft and examine financial reports, statements, and documentation. They manage all aspects of accounting, including tax returns, month-end closeouts, and budgeting, and they provide general financial advice. They also do data entry tasks like recording transactions, documenting and analyzing journal entries, preparing invoices, and making spreadsheets. Additionally, communicating with vendors and customers concerning invoices and payments is a common task for accounting assistants, as well as helping with payroll.
